Mosin-Nagant 28/30

National Origin: Finland

Cartridge: 7.62x54mmR

Feed System: 5-round internal magazine w/ stripper clips

Action: Bolt-action, cock-on-opening

A Finnish improvement upon a Russian design, the Mosin-Nagant 28/30 implemented refinements such as a more finely adjustable sight system, a heavier and more stable barrel, an anti-jam magazine, and a crisper trigger. With its even more heavily modified successor the M39, the Finnish Mosin-Nagants are often considered the most evolved of the Mosin-Nagant design. Alongside the Suomi KP/31 submachine gun, the 28/30 in particular was used by Simo Hayha of the Soviet-Finnish Winter War, to score 505 kills: making him the most successful sniper in military history.
